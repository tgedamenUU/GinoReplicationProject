Welcome

The Gino Replication Project is a multi-year initiative in which student research teams and collaborators systematically replicate the nine studies reported in Gino, Wilmuth & Brooks (2015).

The project is organised into four conceptual blocks:

  • Life goals (Studies 1–2)
  • Desirability vs attainability (Studies 3–4)
  • Expected outcomes of promotion (Studies 5–7)
  • Power & conflict framing (Studies 8–9)

Each block has its own page with study descriptions, a shared replication protocol, and a cumulative overview of results.

Project goals

The project aims to evaluate the robustness of key findings on gender and leadership motivation, train students in open and cumulative science, and build a transparent record of all replications suitable for future meta-analyses.

This project is coordinated by Tom Damen, university lecturer and researcher in Social, Health & Organisational Psychology at Utrecht University. His work focuses on leadership, social identity, and the role of AI and reproducibility in education.
