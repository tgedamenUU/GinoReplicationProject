Before you submit

This page is for student teams and collaborators who have completed a replication study within one of the four blocks (Life goals, Desirability vs attainability, Expected outcomes, Power & conflict).

Before completing the submission form, make sure that:

  • You have followed the official replication protocol for your chosen block as closely as possible.
  • You have created an OSF project or component for your replication.
  • Your data are de-identified and uploaded to OSF.
  • Your analysis code and additional materials are uploaded to OSF.
  • You clearly label exploratory variables and analyses on OSF.

Information requested in the submission form

The online submission form will ask for the following information:

  • Contact person and email address.
  • Institution and course.
  • Year of data collection.
  • Block (Life goals / Desirability vs attainability / Expected outcomes / Power & conflict).
  • Original study number(s) (1–9).
  • Country of data collection.
  • Final sample size used in analyses.
  • Primary effect(s) and effect size(s).
  • Brief replication conclusion (replicated / not replicated / mixed / inconclusive).
  • Link to the OSF project / component.
  • Short description of any additional variables and exploratory analyses.
You can copy these items directly into a Google Form, Qualtrics survey, or another tool of your choice. Paste the final form link in the button below.

Quality criteria for inclusion

All replications included on this website are screened using a short quality checklist. We do not require preregistration. Instead, we use a transparent protocol-compliance check:

  • The official replication protocol for the selected block was followed, or deviations are documented.
  • Original OSF materials were used (survey, coding template, instructions), or justified adaptations are described.
  • Primary analyses follow the protocol.
  • Exploratory variables and analyses are clearly labeled.
  • All materials, data, and code are uploaded to OSF.
  • Sample size is sufficient for the core analyses (flexible across student cohorts).

Student teams complete this checklist at submission; the project coordinator reviews it before adding the replication to the public record.
